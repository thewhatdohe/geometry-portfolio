1.6 — Perpendicular and Parallel Lines

  • Identify perpendicular and parallel lines in diagrams
  • Apply theorems about perpendicular and parallel lines
  • Construct perpendicular bisectors and parallel lines

Core idea: Perpendicular lines intersect at right angles (90°). Parallel lines never intersect and have the same slope. A perpendicular bisector is a line that intersects a segment at its midpoint and forms right angles with it.

Worked example — perpendicular bisector

Q: Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of segment AB with A(2,3) and B(4,7).

  1. Find midpoint: M = ((2+4)/2, (3+7)/2) = (3,5)
  2. Find slope of AB: (7-3)/(4-2) = 4/2 = 2
  3. Perpendicular slope is negative reciprocal: -1/2
  4. Use point-slope form with midpoint: y - 5 = -1/2(x - 3)
  5. Answer: y = -1/2x + 6.5
  1. Are lines with slopes 2 and -1/2 perpendicular? Yes (negative reciprocals).
  2. Find the slope of a line parallel to y = 3x - 5. 3 (same slope).
  3. If line p ⟂ line q and line q ∥ line r, what is the relationship between p and r? p ⟂ r (perpendicular).
Remember: Perpendicular slopes are negative reciprocals. Parallel slopes are equal. Always find the midpoint first when constructing perpendicular bisectors.
