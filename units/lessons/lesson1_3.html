1.3 — Distance and Midpoint Formulas

  • Use the distance formula to find length between two points on a plane
  • Find midpoint coordinates of a segment
  • Understand derivation from Pythagoras

Formulas:

  • Distance: d = √((x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²) — derived from the Pythagorean theorem.
  • Midpoint: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

Worked example — step-by-step

Q: Find distance and midpoint for A(2,3) and B(4,7).

  1. Δx = 4 − 2 = 2; Δy = 7 − 3 = 4.
  2. Distance = √(2² + 4²) = √(4 + 16) = √20 ≈ 4.472.
  3. Midpoint = ((2+4)/2, (3+7)/2) = (3, 5).
  4. Answer: Distance = √20, Midpoint = (3,5).
  1. Find midpoint and distance of G(7,−2), H(−5,−6). Midpoint (1, −4); Distance = 4√10.
