1.2 — Segment Addition, Congruence & Bisectors

  • Apply the Segment Addition Postulate
  • Recognize congruent segments and midpoint
  • Understand segment bisectors and midpoint formula uses

Concept: A segment is the finite piece of a line between two endpoints. If a point B lies between A and C then AB + BC = AC. Congruent segments have equal lengths and we use ≅ to show congruence. A segment bisector intersects a segment at its midpoint.

Worked example — algebra + geometry

Q: AB = 2x, BC = 3x − 4, AC = 11. Find x and lengths.

  1. Use segment addition: AB + BC = AC → 2x + (3x − 4) = 11.
  2. Simplify: 5x − 4 = 11 → 5x = 15 → x = 3.
  3. Compute lengths: AB = 6, BC = 5, check AB + BC = 11 OK.
  4. Answer: x = 3; AB = 6, BC = 5.
  1. If M is midpoint of AB and AM = 5, MB = (2x − 1). Solve x. x = 3.
  2. Given XY ≅ ZW and XY = 7, state ZW. ZW = 7.
  3. Is the midpoint always unique? Explain. Yes — a segment has exactly one midpoint.
Memorize the structure: postulate → equation → solve → check. Always perform a numerical check AB + BC = AC after solving for x.
