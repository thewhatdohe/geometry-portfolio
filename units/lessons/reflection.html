Unit 1 — Reflection (summary)

This unit taught me both content and the habits needed to perform well under exam conditions. Early on I lost marks because I wrote notations incorrectly and rushed. I created a short “notation & formula” reference I study before quizzes: (1) names / labels for points/lines/planes; (2) segment and angle postulates; (3) distance/midpoint formulas.

My study routine that produced improvement:

  1. Daily 10-minute targeted practice (one topic per day).
  2. Active recall: rewrite worked examples without notes and check errors.
  3. Artifact review: upload scanned quizzes and mark where mistakes occurred.

Concrete results: First unit assignment 18/26 → next assessment 10/10 after focused review. I can now solve algebraic angle problems reliably and write basic two-column proofs with correct reasons.


Next steps: Add timed practice: 20 minutes on mixed problems (distance, segment, angles) and two practice proofs each week to build fluency for final exam.
